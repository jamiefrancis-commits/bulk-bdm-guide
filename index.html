The Basics
What is Bulk transport?
Loose, unpackaged materials measured by weight or volume — not boxes or pallets. Think sand, fuel, grain, and waste.
🪨 Dry Bulk
Aggregates, sand, gravel, grain, animal feed, powders
🛢️ Liquid Bulk
Fuels, chemicals, food-grade liquids (often ADR-regulated)
♻️ Waste / Recycling
Muck-away, skips, waste streams requiring licences
Equipment
Specialist kit. Expensive to own.
🚛
Tippers
Aggregates, muck-away, construction materials
🏗️
Grab Lorries
Aggregates and loose materials with hydraulic grab
🛢️
Tankers
Liquids — fuels, chemicals, food-grade
🔄
Walking Floors
Waste, biomass, bulk materials
Most bulk operators own their fleet because the equipment is specialist and compliance-heavy. Typical profile: SME to mid-market, ~15–80 trucks.

What's Different
Bulk is not general haulage
⚖️
Weight is the product
Billing, compliance, and margin hinge on accurate weights and proof
🏗️
Sites dominate the plan
Access restrictions, queues, and site readiness drive daily volatility
📜
Specialist compliance
Waste licences, ADR, food-grade washouts, site safety rules
🔁
High-frequency, short-haul
Many jobs per day — small inefficiencies multiply quickly
Constraints
What keeps operators up at night
⚖️ Weighbridge Accuracy
Gross, tare, and net weights must be precise for billing and compliance
🚫 Vehicle Weight Limits
Axle limits and payload constraints with strict enforcement
🚧 Site Access
Opening hours, one-way systems, wheel-wash, PPE rules, tight yards
⏱️ Waiting Time
Queues at quarries, plants, and tips — weather and site readiness
🧼 Cleaning Rules
Food-grade washouts, contamination prevention for powders and liquids
📋 Regulatory
ADR, waste transfer notes, carrier licensing requirements
Pain Points
Where it hurts most
Manual weighbridge entry
Invoicing errors, disputes, and slow cash collection from re-keying ticket data
Non-billable waiting time
Lost utilisation and margin erosion when waiting isn't captured correctly
Poor site coordination
Wasted driver hours, overtime, and frustrated drivers from wrong instructions
Compliance admin sprawl
Operational risk, fines, failed audits, and lost contracts
Pricing mismatch to reality

Who You Sell To
Know your personas
👤 Owner / MD
Compliance, margin protection, keeping expensive kit utilised. Thinks in risk and return.
📋 Ops / Transport Manager
Site coordination, turnarounds, resource matching, driver efficiency. Lives in the plan.
📅 Planner / Dispatcher
Lots of short jobs, changing site constraints, speed of allocation. Needs fast tools.
💰 Finance / Admin
Weight-based billing accuracy, ticket proof, fewer disputes. Wants clean data fast.
Objection Handling
They'll push back. Be ready.
"We need weighbridge integration."
Reframe: "Agreed. Bulk billing depends on weight proof. Let's map how you capture weights today and what must flow into invoicing."
"Bulk is too messy for software."
Reframe: "Exactly why one system helps. Standardise the repeatable flow, handle exceptions cleanly."
Objection Handling
More pushbacks. More reframes.
"Short-haul, high-volume. Software slows us down."
Reframe: "The goal is speed: faster planning, faster proof capture, fewer admin steps per job."
"Compliance is complex."
Reframe: "Yes. The value is making compliance visible and auditable, without extra admin work."
